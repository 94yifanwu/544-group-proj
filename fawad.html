Shibuya, a special ward in Tokyo, is a very popular destination to visit when traveling to Japan’s eastern side. The area features many fashionable shops, boutiques, and restaurants, providing no end to entertainment and browsing. Several iconic destinations can be found in this shopping district of Tokyo.

In the middle of Shibuya, Shibuya Crossing can be found, one of the most famous scramble crossings in the world, located next to the station. The crossing will occasionally stop all vehicles to allow pedestrians from any side to cross to any other side. The intersection is iconic, with the streets and giant electronic billboards featured in many images and movies.

For a more historical view of the area, one can visit Meiji Shrine, a Shinto shrine around the area. Meiji Shrine has a scenic nature walk through a forest to get to the heart of the shrine, which many visitors may enjoy. One of the most popular shrines in Japan, this destination is a must-visit for any visitors in the area.

A more modern area of Shibuya can be found at Takeshita Street, located in Harajuku, right next to Shibuya. Takeshita Street is a pedestrian shopping district packed with boutiques, eating places, and more. Many of the shops have very wild styles, which may be the start of some fashion trends.

And if you are around Takeshita Street, why not check out Yoyogi Park nearby? Yoyogi park is a popular Tokyo destination to visit, and many groups will use the park as a gathering spot, such as cosplayrs, rock music fans, comedians, and hobby groups. The park spans a wide are with green fields and beautiful trees all around the park. Whether walking through to explore the area, or resting in a shady spot, this area has something for everyone.

Don’t forget to check out these areas if you are in Shibuya, they are definitely worth a visit!
